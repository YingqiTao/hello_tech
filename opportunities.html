Opportunities for young women and non-binary individuals in high school

Click on their names or logos to learn more!

National Center for Women in Technology (NCWIT)

NCWIT is a community of “change leaders focused on advancing innovation by correcting underrepresentation in computing.” They offer programs for professionals, kids, teens, and young adults.

Kode With Klossy

Kode with Klossy offers free (yep, free!) two-week summer programs worldwide for young women and non-binary individuals and teach them how to code or analyze data.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code focuses in keeping girls and young women engaged in STEM fields as they grow by giving them support and a community. It includes after-school clubs, summer courses and programs, and career advice and networking support for college students.

Code.org

A not-for-profit organization, Code.org is dedicated to expanding access to computer science to underrepresented populations including young women. Their Hour Of Code initiative, a free one-hour tutorial designed to provide a fun and easy introduction to coding, is available in 45 languages and has been implemented in over 180 countries.

Built By Girls

Built By Girls provides high school and college women access to real Advisors, exclusive events at top tech companies, and no-fluff resources to help them explore and land their first internship or job.

Black Girls Code

While women are underrepresented in STEM fields, that's even more true for women of color working in the industry. Black Girls Code is an organization that aims to support young and pre-teen girls of color to help give them the resources they need to succeed in STEM fields.

ChickTech

ChickTech provides learning opportunities and technical education in 15 countries around the world. Its high school program offer skill-building workshops and help connect participants to professional networks with formal and informal mentorships

Ada Developers Academy

Named after famed programmer Ada Lovelace, Ada Developers Academy offers a unique, immersive experience for women looking to break into tech. Their free, 6-month training program teaches participants the fundamentals of computer science and programming with languages including Python, JavaScript, and SQL.
