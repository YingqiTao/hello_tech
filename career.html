Careers in Technology

Software Developers

Software developers create softwares, such as every apps on your phone and every websites you browse. They affect virtually every part of our lives -- and definitely our virtual lives.

Data Scientist

Data scientist study data, as you would expect. Instead of a microscope, they use technology as their tools for sifting through the vast amounts of numbers and research to figure out what all the data means.

Sales Engineers

Sales enginers sell complex scientific and technological products or services to companies. They use their technical skills to explain the benefits of their products or services to potential customers and to show how their products or services are better than their competitors.

Project Manager

Project managers are in charge of the entire project and handles everything involved, such as planning, managing software developers, and allocating resources. They help ensure the software project can be completed in time.

Information Security Analyst

Information security analysts are essentially high-tech security guards. But instead of standing for hours and chasing the occasional thief, they protect people's information systems from hackers and cyberattacks.

Computer Systems Analyst

Computer systems analysts design more efficient computer systems and processes for clients. They need to find the best technology to help the company's computer system. They also oversee the installation, testing, and training of the new system.

Computer Network Architect

Computer network architects design, build, and maintain the digital world you live in. They create numerous types of data communication networks, such as cloud infrastructures to smaller intranets.

Database administrator

Database administrator isn't a term we hear a lot, but there are a lot of them, and there's demand for more. DBAs organize a company's database. They essentially run the digital side of an office. They work with software developers to ensure that a database isn't compromised by hackers.

Technical Support Specialist

If you've had trouble logging into a computer, if the software has gone haywire, or if your operating system is down, you may have called a technical support specialist. Technical support specialists help frustrated people who want to use technologies.
